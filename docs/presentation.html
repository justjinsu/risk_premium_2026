Quantifying Climate Risk Premium

A Case Study of Samcheok Blue Power

Risk-Efficiency Theory of Corporate Decarbonization

The Challenge

  • Unpriced Risks: Infrastructure projects face significant climate risks often ignored by traditional models.
  • Transition Risks:
    • Carbon taxes & pricing.
    • Early retirement policies (Coal Phase-out).
    • Policy shifts (e.g., 11th Basic Plan).
  • Physical Risks:
    • Wildfires disrupting transmission.
    • Droughts affecting cooling water availability.
    • Efficiency losses due to heat stress.

Our Solution

A Modular, Data-Driven Financial Analysis Tool

  • Quantifies Climate Risk Premium (CRP): Translates qualitative risks into quantitative financial impacts.
  • Holistic Modeling: Integrates both transition and physical risks.
  • Actionable Insights: Outputs impacts on NPV, IRR, and Credit Ratings.
  • Risk-Adjusted Valuation: Enables smarter investment and divestment decisions.

Methodology

1. Inputs: Plant Parameters, Scenarios, Financing Data.

⬇️

2. Risk Engine: Applies penalties (Carbon Tax, Derating) & Adjusts Cash Flows.

⬇️

3. Outputs: Financial Metrics (NPV, IRR) & Risk Metrics (CRP, Expected Loss).

Case Study: Samcheok Blue Power

  • Asset: 2,100 MW Ultra-Supercritical Coal Power Plant.
  • Significance: The last coal plant built in South Korea.
  • Status: Commercial Operation started in 2024.
  • Investment: ~4.9 Trillion KRW.
  • Key Risk: Stranded Asset Risk due to accelerated coal phase-out targets (e.g., 2040).

Scenario Analysis

We model multiple futures to bound the risk:

  • Baseline: Business as usual (No climate impact).
  • Transition Scenarios:
    • Moderate: Aligned with current NDCs.
    • Aggressive: Net Zero 2050 (High Carbon Price, Early Retirement).
  • Physical Scenarios:
    • Moderate: RCP 4.5.
    • High: RCP 8.5 (Extreme weather events).
  • Combined: Worst-case "Perfect Storm".

Key Findings: Financial Impact

  • NPV Erosion: Significant value destruction in Aggressive Transition scenarios.
  • Cost of Capital: Climate Risk Premium (CRP) spikes, increasing financing costs.
  • Credit Rating Migration:
    • Quantitative assessment using KIS methodology.
    • Potential downgrades (e.g., AA- → A or BBB).
    • Risk of falling below Investment Grade.

Interactive Dashboard

Our Streamlit-based application allows stakeholders to:

  • Simulate Scenarios: Real-time adjustment of risk parameters.
  • Visualize Impacts: Cash Flow Waterfalls, Capacity Factor Trajectories, NPV Comparisons.
  • Deep Dive: Drill down into specific financial years and metrics.

Conclusion

  • Climate Risk is Financial Risk.
  • Ignoring these factors leads to mispricing of assets and capital.
  • Our model provides the rigorous, quantitative framework needed for:
    • Accurate Asset Valuation.
    • Strategic Decarbonization Planning.
    • Resilient Portfolio Management.
